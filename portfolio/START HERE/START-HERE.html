THE ASSIGNMENT

CONTEXT

As part of my graduation internship, I had the opportunity to work on a project focused on blockchain technology and the privacy concerns of DAO users. DAOs are decentralized organizations that operate using smart contracts on a blockchain, and one of the key challenges in this space is ensuring that user privacy is protected. My internship involved researching and proposing solutions to address these privacy issues, with a focus on finding ways to balance the need for transparency and accountability within DAOs with the need to protect sensitive user data. It was a challenging and rewarding experience that allowed me to delve deeply into the technical and societal implications of blockchain technology and DAOs

PROBLEM DESCRIPTION

Tooling for DAOs is in active development and new improvements and solutions are introduced regularly. One of the main DAO features is that they want to be completely transparent, but DAO members may want to stay anonymous and still be a part of this organization. DAOs use smart contracts to work in a blockchain. These smart contracts are made of chunks of code that help execute operations automatically when a set of criteria are met. While Ethereum was the first blockchain to use smart contracts, it is deployed on various other blockchains. Many blockchain networks operate as public databases, meaning anyone with an Internet connection can view a list of the network's transaction history. This leads to the issue of low privacy because users of DAOs may not want their activities such as voting or proposing to be displayed to everyone on the network. Currently, Byont has not enough resources to look into possible solutions. However, they still want to conduct research and do some possible development of this technology for their projects.

THE GOALS

The main goals of my graduation internship were to research and develop solutions for improving privacy for users within DAOs. This involved identifying the key privacy challenges faced by DAO users, researching available tooling and technologies that could be used to address these challenges, and designing and prototyping potential solutions. As part of the internship, I also worked to provide advice and guidance on the best approaches for addressing privacy concerns within the context of DAOs. Overall, the goal of the internship was to advise and show Byont the possible tooling and solutions for solving privacy issues of the users within DAOs.

SOLUTION

The final products of this internship will be research documents, blogs, and advice on the possible solutions for improving user privacy in DAOs, with a focus on conducting research and development on technologies and approaches that can improve privacy. This may involve writing research documents and blogs to explore the various options available, as well as providing advice and guidance on the best approaches for addressing privacy concerns. In addition to this, proof of concept and prototypes will be submitted to Byont, with the goal of engaging in prototyping and testing of possible technologies and solutions to see how they perform in practice. If possible, prototype implementation for current projects of Byont should be carried out as well. By doing this, Byont can help to advance the state of the art in this area and contribute to the development of better tooling and approaches for ensuring privacy within DAOs.

REFLECTION

The graduation internship project started off very well, with everything going according to plan and meeting the initial goals. However, as time passed and the complexity and scope of the project grew, it became increasingly difficult to follow the project plan and maintain focus on the initial goals. One of the main challenges I faced was the technology I was working with, which was very new and involved an academic level of mathematical knowledge and cryptography that I did not have. This made it difficult to fully understand the technical aspects of the project, and it also impacted the planning process as I struggled to grasp the concepts and techniques being used.

In addition to this, the research phase of the project took longer than expected, as I had to spend extra time learning about the technology and trying to get up to speed. Despite my best efforts, I am not entirely proud of the results of the project, and I feel that more could have been done to add value for the company.

Despite these challenges, I am proud of this internship for a number of reasons. One of the biggest risks I took was working with new, emerging technologies, which can be both exciting and challenging. I learned a lot about the professional skills required to be successful in the ICT domain, and I gained valuable experience working on a real-world project.

One of the most important things I learned as an ICT professional is the importance of gathering user requirements from stakeholders which I wish I did more properly. This is crucial in order to ensure that the project is meeting the needs of the users and delivering value. I learned the importance of asking the right questions and listening carefully to the responses, as well as being proactive in seeking out feedback and input from stakeholders. Communication with stakeholders is also key, as it helps to keep everyone on the same page and ensure that the project is moving in the right direction. This was a big learning point for me

Another important lesson I learned is the importance of timing. As an ICT professional, it is essential to be able to accurately measure the time needed to solve a problem or implement a particular solution. This helps to ensure that projects stay on track and are completed within the allocated time and resources. I learned the value of creating detailed project plans and schedules, and the importance of regularly reviewing and updating these plans to account for changing circumstances and priorities.

In addition to the technical and professional skills I gained during this internship, I also learned a lot about myself and how I work best. I discovered that I enjoy being challenged and pushed out of my comfort zone. I also learned that I am more resilient and capable than I had previously thought, and that I can handle difficult situations and setbacks with grace and determination.

Overall, this internship has been a valuable learning experience for me, and I am grateful for the opportunity to work on such a challenging and rewarding project. While there were certainly some difficulties and setbacks along the way, I believe that I have grown both professionally and personally as a result of this experience. I have gained new skills, knowledge, and confidence, and I am excited to apply these lessons as I move forward in my career.
